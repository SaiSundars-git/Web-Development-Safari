ZAMBIA

Overview


Often cast aside as a destination for seasoned safari travellers, Zambia is an incredible destination for new safari goers alike. The country boasts 20 national parks, the incredible Victoria Falls, some of the best big game viewing in Africa. The huge areas of raw wilderness and stunning scenery are a true reflection of the untamed Africa. For those wanting a walking Safari Zambia is the go to and is actually known as the “home of the walking Safari”.


WILDLIFE IN ZAMBIA


Zambia’s wildlife is diverse and abundant. The big 5 inhabit most of the parks but rhino are incredibly rare and confined to two parks. Aside from big game, the country’s many parks boast an array of incredible endemic species such as the black lechwe, Crawshay’s zebra and Thornicroft’s Giraffe. The Zambezi River is home to huge numbers of crocodiles and Hippos as well as the rare Tigerfish. In general, May to October is the best time for game viewing as the climate is drier and animals are more easily spotted but Zambia is a prime destination for wildlife enthusiasts April through to December.
